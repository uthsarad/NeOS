# Risk & Priority Report

## Current Risk Assessment
We are currently accumulating a highly concerning, deeply impactful, and fundamentally unacceptable level of validation debt that threatens the very core of our architectural stability and product timeline. Strict systemd sandboxing has recently been aggressively applied to our critical infrastructure services (`neos-autoupdate.service` and `neos-liveuser-setup.service`); however, mandatory specialist reviews—Sentinel for rigorous security privilege auditing and Palette for verifying logging UX and error clarity—remain pending and completely unverified as explicitly logged in the respective `ai/tasks/*.json` task manifests.

**Identified Risks:**
1. **Compounding Validation Debt:** The core service hardening has not yet been audited by our critical specialists, leaving a substantial, critical, and entirely unacceptable gap in our quality assurance pipeline. Moving forward without clearing this specific debt guarantees dangerous blind spots in security, stability, and system reliability. Every single day this debt remains, the risk compounds exponentially.
2. **Regression Blindspots in Sandboxing:** Unverified strict systemd sandboxing directives (e.g., `ProtectSystem=strict`, `ProtectHome=yes`) may silently and catastrophically break critical operations. Specifically, applying `ProtectSystem=strict` to `neos-liveuser-setup.service` creates a severe regression vector where initialization scripts may be denied write access to necessary directories unless `ReadWritePaths` are explicitly and exhaustively configured, breaking the live environment entirely. `useradd -m` requires access to `/etc` lockfiles and `/home` specifically. Furthermore, pacman autoupdates require unfettered access to `/var/lib/pacman`, `/etc/pacman.d`, and `/usr/lib/modules` for DKMS rebuilds, which also strictly requires preserving CAP_SYS_MODULE and access to /opt and /srv. We cannot rely on assumptions when core system operability is at stake.
3. **Feature Creep & Instability:** Pursuing any further Phase 1, Phase 2, or Phase 3 Roadmap features before rigorously validating our active infrastructure hardening creates severe, compounding instability risks. It severely complicates subsequent troubleshooting efforts, and moves us dangerously further away from a secure, dependably stable baseline. It represents a fundamental failure in engineering discipline and project management.
